version: 1
scenario: >-
  You're opening a new cake shop next month. How should you set it up so the
  business can work without depending entirely on you?
options:
  - id: a
    content: >-
      Design the business as a working model first — define recipes, roles,
      cleaning standards, buying process, customer experience, hiring methods,
      and training systems before opening.
    pros:
      - treats the cake shop itself as the product, not just the cakes
      - creates repeatable systems so quality does not depend on the owner
      - makes it easier to hire and train people later
      - aligns with how mature franchises like McDonald's are built
    cons:
      - requires significant planning time before opening
      - less room to adapt the model to early customer feedback
  - id: b
    content: >-
      Open the shop and do the baking, selling, and cleanup yourself; build
      systems only when a problem forces you to.
    pros:
      - lets you get to opening faster
      - stays flexible to learn what customers actually want
    cons:
      - keeps the owner trapped doing the daily work
      - quality and experience vary day to day
      - the business cannot run or grow without the owner
:::
